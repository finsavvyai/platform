# An Open Letter to the Software Community

## From the Founders of UPM

---

February 14, 2024

Dear Friends, Colleagues, and Fellow Builders,

Three years ago, we watched in horror as the SolarWinds attack unfolded. A single compromised dependency. 18,000+ organizations affected. Hundreds of millions of dollars in damage. And the scary part? It could have been any of us.

That was the moment we decided: **Enough.**

---

## The Problem We're Solving

Today, 97% of applications contain open-source code. The average enterprise app has 5,000+ dependencies. Yet most organizations have visibility into less than 10% of what's actually running in production.

This is madness.

**We're building skyscrapers on foundations we've never inspected.**

The Log4Shell vulnerability should have been a wake-up call. A single logging library—used by 35% of all Java applications—nearly broke the internet. And what did we do? We panicked. We scrambled. We patched manually for weeks. We lost sleep. We lost money. And we prayed the next one wouldn't be worse.

It will be worse. Unless we do something different.

---

## Our Solution: UPM

Today, we're announcing UPM—the Universal Dependency Platform.

**UPM is not another scanner.** You have enough of those.

**UPM is not another dashboard.** You don't need more graphs.

**UPM is not another tool to learn.** Your team is already overwhelmed.

**UPM is the first platform that actually fixes the problem.**

Here's what makes UPM different:

### 1. Universal
One platform for every ecosystem. Maven, npm, PyPI, Cargo, Go, PHP, NuGet—all of it. No more fragmentation. No more tool sprawl.

### 2. Intelligent
Our AI doesn't just find vulnerabilities. It predicts which ones will actually be exploited. It tells you what matters, so you can focus on what's important.

### 3. Automated
UPM doesn't just alert—it fixes. One-click remediation with breaking-change detection, test validation, and automatic rollback. What takes weeks today takes minutes.

### 4. Developer-First
Security that your developers will actually love. Real-time alerts in their IDE. Context-aware recommendations. Zero friction. Invisible security.

### 5. Always On
Continuous monitoring. Instant alerts for new CVEs affecting your dependencies. You'll know before the attackers do.

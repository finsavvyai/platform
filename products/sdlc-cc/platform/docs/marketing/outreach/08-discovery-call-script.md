# Template 08 — 30-Minute Discovery Call Script (Inbound Leads)

**Status:** Reference script for the human on the call. Do not read verbatim.

---

## Call notes for the founder

- **Goal of this call:** understand the prospect's problem well enough to know whether sdlc.cc fits. *Not* to close. If it doesn't fit, say so and refer them out.
- **Format:** 30 minutes, video preferred. Camera on. No slides unless they ask.
- **Recording:** ask consent at the top. If they decline, take written notes instead. Never record covertly.
- **Talk ratio:** target 30% you, 70% them. If you're talking more, you're losing.
- **Send a summary email within 24 hours** with action items and any technical materials you promised.

---

## Minute 0–3 — Opening

> "Thanks for making time, {first_name}. Before I jump in — I usually record these for my own notes only, not shared. Is that okay, or would you rather I just write things down? … Got it.
>
> Quick frame for the next 30: I'd like to spend most of it understanding what you're working on and where AI fits in. I'll save the product walk-through for the end, and only if it's relevant. Sound good?"

If they push back on the frame, follow their lead — some buyers want the demo first. That's fine.

---

## Minute 3–10 — Their context

Open-ended questions, in this order. Skip any that they've already answered.

1. "Tell me about your role at {firm_name} and what 'AI strategy' means in your seat day-to-day."
2. "When you say your firm is looking at AI tools, what's the actual prompt — a client demanding it, a partner asking, a competitive concern, or something else?"
3. "What have you already tried or evaluated? Doesn't have to be a yes-no list — I'm curious what worked and what didn't."
4. "Where does confidentiality / privilege sit in the decision? Is your firm taking the 'never touch a vendor' line, or is it more nuanced?"

Take notes. Repeat key phrases back in their words ("So the issue isn't cost, it's the engagement-letter language with your healthcare clients — did I get that right?").

---

## Minute 10–18 — The technical detail

If they've signaled they want depth:

1. "What's your current stack? Where does data live — on-prem, AWS, Azure, mixed?"
2. "Who owns IT at the firm? Is there an internal team that can run infra, or is everything outsourced?"
3. "What does your audit / compliance review process look like for a new tool? Months or weeks?"
4. "Have you read ABA Op. 512? If yes, what stuck with you? If no, no worries — short version is it makes competence and confidentiality explicit obligations when using GenAI."

Cite Op. 512 here once if it hasn't come up — don't repeat it later.

---

## Minute 18–25 — Show, briefly

Only if it's relevant. If they're 18 months from a decision, skip the demo and offer to send the architecture doc instead.

If you do demo:

- Architecture diagram for 60 seconds — the boundary line is the only thing that matters
- Audit log walk-through for 90 seconds — show one real request end-to-end
- DLP redaction for 60 seconds — show the before/after
- Stop. Do not show every feature. Ask: "Does this map to what you were imagining, or is it different?"

---

## Minute 25–30 — Close honestly

1. "Based on what you've told me — and I'm going to be direct — does this feel like it fits, or are we early?"
2. If fit: "What's the next step on your side? Who else needs to see this, and what would they want to know that I haven't covered?"
3. If not fit: "I don't want to waste your time. It sounds like {real_reason}. If you want, I can point you to {real_alternative}." Then actually do it.
4. Always offer: "I'll send a summary of what we discussed within 24 hours with the architecture doc and anything else you asked for. Anything you want me to add to that note?"

---

## What never to do on this call

- Quote a price without understanding scope
- Promise a feature that doesn't exist
- Claim a customer or reference you don't have
- Push for a same-week decision
- Disparage a competitor by name (acknowledge them, differentiate technically, move on)
- Send a contract before they've asked for one

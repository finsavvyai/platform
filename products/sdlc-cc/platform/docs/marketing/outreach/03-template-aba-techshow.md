# Template 03 — Outreach: ABA TECHSHOW Speakers & Panel Chairs

**Status:** Draft for manual send. Do not automate.

---

## Sender notes

- **Target:** Past and upcoming ABA TECHSHOW speakers, track chairs on the AI / Cybersecurity / Practice Management tracks, and members of the ABA Law Practice Division Council. Source from the public TECHSHOW program PDF and the ABA member-facing speaker bios.
- **Volume:** 5–10 sends per week. These are senior people. Quality beats quantity.
- **Expected response rate:** 10–20% if you've actually attended TECHSHOW or have a real connection to their session.
- **Do not** claim ABA endorsement. Do not imply you're a TECHSHOW exhibitor unless you've paid for booth space. Do not write "ABA-approved" anywhere.

---

## Subject line

`Your {techshow_year} session on {session_topic} — follow-up question on self-hosted AI`

---

## Body

Hi {first_name},

I caught your {techshow_year} TECHSHOW session on {session_topic} (or read the recap on {source}, if I missed it live). Your point about {specific_point_they_made} stuck with me, and I've been thinking about it from the infrastructure side ever since.

I'm a software engineer, not a lawyer. I build sdlc.cc, an open-source RAG and LLM gateway that firms can deploy inside their own cloud tenancy so privileged data never reaches a vendor. The reason I'm reaching out: I'd value 20 minutes of your time as a practitioner, not as a sales call.

Specifically:

1. When you're advising firms on GenAI procurement under Rule 1.6 and Op. 512, what's the single question that trips them up most?
2. Is "self-hosted vs. vendor-hosted" a real decision point for the firms you work with, or are most defaulting to a single approved vendor?
3. Would a written technical brief on the self-hosted approach (no product pitch, just architecture and trade-offs) be useful to your audience or your CLE materials? I'm happy to write one and let you use it freely.

I'm not asking you to recommend the product, speak at anything, or endorse a vendor. I'd like to learn from your view of the market and offer something back in return.

If this isn't a fit or the timing's wrong, no problem at all — reply "pass" and I won't follow up.

Best,
Shahar Solomon
Founder, sdlc.cc
{sender_email}
{mailing_address}

Reply "remove" to opt out of any further outreach.

## What to swap

- `{first_name}`, `{techshow_year}`, `{session_topic}`, `{specific_point_they_made}` — required and real

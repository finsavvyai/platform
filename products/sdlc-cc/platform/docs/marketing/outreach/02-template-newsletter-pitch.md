# Template 02 — Pitch: Legal-Tech Newsletter Editors

**Status:** Draft for manual send. Do not automate.

---

## Sender notes

- **Target editors:**
  - Bob Ambrogi — *LawSites* / *LawNext* podcast
  - Joe Patrice / Kathryn Rubino — *Above the Law*
  - Editorial desk — *Legaltech News* (ALM) and *Law360 Pulse*
  - Niki Black — *MyCase / Above the Law* tech column
- **Volume:** Send individually. Five well-researched pitches per week max. Never CC multiple editors on the same email.
- **Expected response rate:** 5–15% if the pitch is genuinely newsworthy. Higher if you can offer an exclusive angle.
- **Lead with the story, not the product.** Editors do not owe you coverage. They owe their readers something useful.

---

## Subject line

`Story pitch: self-hosted AI for firms post-ABA Op. 512 — happy to give you the technical walkthrough`

---

## Body

Hi {first_name},

Long-time reader. Your {recent_article_title} piece on {topic} was the cleanest summary I've seen of where firms actually are with GenAI adoption — not the marketing version.

I'm writing because I'm working on a story angle I think fits {publication_name}, and I'd rather pitch it directly than pretend it's a cold press release.

**The angle:** Most coverage of legal AI focuses on either (a) the big-vendor names (Harvey, Thomson Reuters CoCounsel, Lexis+ AI) or (b) the malpractice horror stories (Mata v. Avianca, the Michael Cohen case). There's a third lane that's underreported: open-source, self-hostable AI infrastructure that lets a firm keep privileged data inside its own tenancy. ABA Formal Opinion 512 essentially makes this a procurement question for every firm with an AI committee, but most coverage stops at "firms are worried about confidentiality."

I run a small project — sdlc.cc — in that lane. AGPL-3.0 licensed, with a commercial license for firms that need one. I'm not asking you to cover the product. I'm offering to walk you through the architecture, the audit-log design, and the procurement questions firms are actually asking. Use any of it, none of it, or attribute it to "a vendor in the space."

If it's useful, I can also introduce you to two attorneys (not clients — peers) who are evaluating self-hosted approaches and would talk on background.

20 minutes by phone or video, your schedule. If this isn't your beat, no worries — and please reply "remove" if you'd rather I don't pitch you again.

Best,
Shahar Solomon
Founder, sdlc.cc
{sender_email}
{mailing_address}

To opt out of future pitches from me, reply "remove."

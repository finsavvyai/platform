**Generated**: 2026-04-09

## Key Findings

### Who: 4 clear segments
1. **Solo Developers** (40%) — Free tier, love AI test generation
2. **QA Team Leads** (25%) — Starter/Pro, value self-healing most
3. **Engineering Managers** (20%) — Pro, care about CI/CD + analytics
4. **Enterprise QA** (15%) — Custom pricing, need SSO + audit + compliance

### What Sticks: Self-healing is the moat
The self-healing engine (9.2/10 stickiness) is the single biggest retention driver. Once tests auto-heal, switching cost becomes enormous. AI test generation (8.5/10) is the acquisition hook, but self-healing is what prevents churn.

### Revenue: 80/20 rule applies
Pro + Enterprise = 18% of users but 82% of revenue. Free tier is pure acquisition. The key conversion point is Free → Starter (when users hit 100 runs/month).

### Growth: Content + community
Best acquisition channels are organic (Dev.to, GitHub, SEO). Enterprise requires direct outreach. Referral program with CI badges ("Tested with Qestro") could drive viral adoption.

### Churn: Feature gaps + complexity
30% churn from missing features (visual regression, load testing). 25% from complexity (features they never discovered). Fix: better onboarding + feature discovery emails.

# Autify (autify.com)

> **Category**: Incumbent / codeless AI testing, strong Japan market
> **Threat level to Qestro**: **MEDIUM** — they've pivoted hard to "agentic AI testing" (Aximo) and have real product surface

## 1. One-sentence positioning
"Accelerate and simplify the entire Software Testing Lifecycle through AI" — with Aximo as their autonomous AI testing agent.

## 2. Core product capabilities
- **Autify Aximo**: autonomous AI testing agent using natural language + visual recognition
- **Autify Nexus**: next-gen platform built on Playwright (direct overlap with Qestro)
- **Autify Genesis**: GenAI test case + code generation
- **Autify Mobile**: no-code mobile test automation
- Self-healing locators (flexible locator system)
- Cross-platform: web, mobile, desktop
- On-premises deployment option (Enterprise)

## 3. What they do BETTER than Qestro
- **Four product SKUs shipped** (Aximo, Nexus, Genesis, Mobile) — more surface area than Qestro today.
- **Japan market dominance**: very strong in APAC, which Qestro isn't addressing.
- **Desktop app testing** included — Qestro doesn't.
- **On-prem option on Enterprise** — for regulated customers.
- **GenAI test generation** is a named product (Genesis), which matches Qestro's marketing angle 1:1.
- **Aximo agentic approach** — closer to Qestro's vision than most incumbents.

## 4. What Qestro does BETTER than them
- **Simpler offering**. Autify has 4 product names; prospects have to figure out which one they need. Qestro is one product, one dashboard.
- **Transparent pricing**. Autify Aximo Free → $120/mo Core → $550/mo Team is reasonable, but Nexus is priced per-user ($3,600/yr for a single user) which gets expensive fast.
- **Playwright-native code output** — Nexus is built on Playwright but output fidelity to raw Playwright is unclear. Qestro emits standard Playwright.
- **Self-healing is integrated across all runners**, not a feature-flag on one SKU.
- **Cloudflare edge latency** for the dashboard vs Autify's regional hosting.

## 5. Tech stack signals
- Autify Nexus built on Playwright (they confirm this publicly)
- Aximo likely uses vision models + LLM reasoning (self-described "visual recognition")
- Backend: unknown, likely Node.js/Go based on hiring pages
- HQ in San Francisco + Tokyo

## 7. Target customer
Mid-market to enterprise teams in APAC (strong Japan presence), US enterprise accounts for Aximo. Mix of QA engineers and product engineers. Less developer-native than Qestro targets.

## 8. Recent signals
- Heavy push on agentic AI testing in 2026 — Aximo is their flagship repositioning.
- Multiple product launches in the last year — they're iterating fast.
- Webinars on "agentic AI testing" — clearly chasing the same narrative as Qestro.

## 9. Qestro's winning angle vs Autify

**Autify's product strategy is confused because they're straddling two worlds**: the legacy codeless tester and the new agentic AI tester. They ship 4 SKUs to try to cover both. Qestro is just one product that does the new thing.

"Autify asks you to choose between their legacy codeless tool, their Playwright wrapper, their mobile tool, or their new AI agent. Qestro is one thing — an AI-first testing platform across browser, mobile, and API, built on Playwright. One price, one dashboard, one product to learn."

**Key wedge plays vs Autify**:
1. **Product simplicity**: one product vs four.
2. **Developer buyer**: Autify pitches QA directors. Qestro pitches the engineer.
3. **US/EU focus**: Autify's APAC strength isn't defensible in SF or London.
4. **Unbundled pricing**: Autify Nexus charges $250/mo per additional user. Qestro's seats scale with the tier.

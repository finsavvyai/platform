# QA Wolf (qawolf.com)

> **Category**: Managed QA service with human + AI hybrid, SOC2
> **Threat level to Qestro**: **MEDIUM-HIGH** — they target companies that don't want to run QA themselves, which is a different buyer but overlapping budget

## 1. One-sentence positioning
"The AI testing platform for fast, fearless releases" — hybrid platform + service with embedded full-time QA engineers + AI agents that map applications, write tests, and execute in parallel.

## 2. Core product capabilities
- Coverage-as-a-Service: embedded full-time QA engineers on your team
- AI agents that auto-map applications and generate tests
- Unlimited parallel test execution
- Unlimited test maintenance (human + AI)
- Video playback per test run
- Zero-flake guarantee (contractual)
- Platforms: Web, Electron, iOS, Android, Salesforce
- Extended testing: Canvas APIs, iBeacon, barcode scanning, GenAI eval (LLM-as-judge), email/SMS, phone calls, a11y, visual regression, performance
- SOC 2 certified

## 3. What they do BETTER than Qestro
- **Full managed service**: they write and maintain the tests for you. Qestro is self-serve.
- **Contractual zero-flake guarantee** — that's a huge value prop for VP Engineering buyers.
- **Unusual surface area**: phone calls, SMS, iBeacon, barcode scanning, GenAI eval. Qestro doesn't touch those.
- **Salesforce + Electron + iOS + Android**: broader platform coverage than Qestro today.
- **Enterprise case studies**: Salesloft ($750K savings), Metronome, Drata, Lifesum, UserTesting — strong logos.

## 4. What Qestro does BETTER than them
- **Self-serve and 100x cheaper**. QA Wolf's median ACV is ~$90K; enterprise deals are $180K-$250K+/yr. Qestro Starter is $99/mo ($1,188/yr). Entirely different buyer.
- **Developer owns the tests**. With QA Wolf, an external team owns your test suite and you're locked in to keep paying. With Qestro, your team owns the Playwright code in your own repo.
- **Instant velocity**. QA Wolf onboarding is weeks-to-months (they need to learn your app). Qestro onboarding is minutes.
- **No lock-in**. Leave QA Wolf and you lose the QA team. Leave Qestro and you keep your Playwright test suite.
- **Transparent**: QA Wolf pricing is quote-based. Qestro tiers are on the website.

## 5. Tech stack signals
- Backend: proprietary orchestrator, likely Node.js + Playwright under the hood
- Services layer with heavy concurrency (unlimited parallel execution)
- SOC 2 Type II + HIPAA-aligned posture
- No public GitHub for their product

## 6. Pricing tiers
- **No public pricing.** Contact-sales only.
- Third-party sources (Vendr, G2, Bug0) cite:
  - Median ACV: ~$90,000/year
  - Per-test pricing band: $40-$44 per test per month
  - Enterprise (800+ tests, multi-platform): $180K-$250K+/year
- No free tier.

## 7. Target customer
Series C+ startups and mid-market companies that have outgrown DIY QA but don't want to hire a QA team. Heavy in fast-moving SaaS (4+ deploys/day). CFO-level buyers because of the ACV.

## 8. Recent signals
- Active blog, strong thought leadership content ("QA Wolf is reinventing QA pricing", 2025).
- Consistent case study cadence — they're selling.
- SOC 2 + HIPAA-aligned — enterprise-ready.
- No public funding news recently — runway status unknown but seems healthy.

## 9. Qestro's winning angle vs QA Wolf

**These are not the same buyer.** QA Wolf's buyer is "I want to outsource QA entirely and I have $90K/yr to spend." Qestro's buyer is "I want AI to write my tests for me and I want to pay $99/mo on a credit card."

If a prospect is evaluating QA Wolf, they've already decided they want a managed service. Qestro's pitch there is:

"QA Wolf is a great managed service if you want to outsource QA. Qestro is the tool your team uses if you want to keep QA in-house but get 10x leverage from AI. We're a tool, not a service. You'll pay 50x less — but you'll also need to run it yourself. That's the right trade-off for engineering-led teams."

**Key wedge plays vs QA Wolf**:
1. **Price anchor**: "QA Wolf is $90K/yr. Qestro Pro is $499/mo. That's $84K/yr of savings for your team to own QA themselves."
2. **Code ownership**: "Your tests shouldn't live on someone else's SaaS. Qestro generates Playwright code you own."
3. **No lock-in**: QA Wolf churn = lose your test suite. Qestro churn = keep your Playwright files.
4. **Velocity**: weeks to ramp QA Wolf. 5 minutes to ramp Qestro.

**Where Qestro loses to QA Wolf**: a VP Engineering at a Series C who'd rather pay $150K/yr than hire two QA engineers. We don't want to fight there.

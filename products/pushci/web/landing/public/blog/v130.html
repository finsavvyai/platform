Where's the Bottleneck?

I asked my DevOps guy where the bottleneck was. He said "somewhere in the build."

Somewhere.

In the build.

That's like a doctor saying the problem is "somewhere in your body." Thank you. Very helpful. I'll just fix… all of it, I guess.

pushci run --trace

One command. Generates Chrome Trace Event JSON. Drag it into ui.perfetto.dev and see exactly which step took 47 seconds while the rest finished in 3.

No other CI tool does this. Zero. I checked. They all give you "Build: 2m 34s" and expect you to figure it out. Like giving someone a receipt total and asking them to work backwards to what they ordered.

The Flaky Test Conversation

You know this meeting. Everyone's been in this meeting.

The test passed on your machine. It passed in CI. It passed 9 out of 10 times. But on that tenth time, the whole team stops what they're doing for a meeting about "test reliability."

Two hours later, someone says "let's just re-run it." Two hours. For "re-run it."

pushci run --stress 10

Runs each check 10 times. Color-coded flake rate report. Now you have proof that test_payment_webhook fails 30% of the time instead of everyone pretending it doesn't.

You can walk into that meeting, put the report on the table, and say "It's this one. It's been this one the whole time. Can I go back to work now?"

Everything Runs in Parallel Now

Build, test, security scan, deploy. All running simultaneously on isolated git worktrees. Not sequentially. Not "after the build finishes." At the same time.

Two strategies: race (first agent to finish wins) or consensus (all agents must agree). Your CI pipeline just became a parallel processing system instead of a sad, sequential queue where each step waits politely for the previous one to finish, like British people at a bus stop.
